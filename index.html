mission

core vision

Not a club. A unit.

ATLAS{0D} is not a casual community. It is a compact, disciplined team that trains like a real cyber operations unit: small, focused, and driven by improvement.

long-term goal

Compete at a world level.

The objective is to reach international standards: CTFs, labs, tooling, research, and real-world skills in both offensive and defensive security.

team specialties

Red Team

Offensive operations, exploitation, adversary simulation and attack-paths against modern infrastructures.

SOC / Blue Team

Detection engineering, log analysis, incident monitoring, alert triage and response playbooks.

DFIR & Malware

Forensics, timeline building, memory & disk analysis, malware behaviour and reverse-engineering basics.

Cloud & AppSec

Hardening, misconfig detection, secure design, web/app exploitation and remediation patterns.

DevSecOps

CI/CD security, secrets hygiene, supply-chain awareness and automation for secure delivery.

AI × Cyber

Using AI to support defense and understanding how models can be attacked, abused or misused.

15-day challenge cycle

Grow or stay behind.

  • Every 15 days, each operator receives a challenge in their field.
  • Challenges are adapted to level: beginner → advanced.
  • Failure gives a short recovery window to fix and learn.
  • Repeated inactivity or lack of effort = removal from the unit.

> ATLAS{0D} is designed for people who want to move forward, not for spectators.

opsec & anonymity

Identity rules.

  • Alias only — no real names, age, nationality or personal links.
  • No real-face profile pictures, no social media connections.
  • Do not ask others about their real identity. Ever.

Purpose of OPSEC.

OPSEC is part of the training itself: anonymity, discipline and compartmentalisation are core skills for any serious operator.

how to join

Entry is selective, but open.

  • Fill the official ATLAS{0D} Entry Form.
  • We evaluate mindset, logic, motivation and focus.
  • We accept a small number of operators per specialty.
  • Beginners are welcome if they are serious about learning.
